About SK Ratibor Club

SK Ratibor is a sports club that combines wrestling and hand-to-hand combat disciplines. We create an environment where athletes develop strength, technique, and character regardless of their level of training. The foundation of the training process is discipline, respect, and a systematic approach to learning. The club focuses on both basic technique and competitive preparation: from the first steps on the mat to participation in tournaments. Training includes elements of wrestling, striking techniques, and functional preparation, which helps develop endurance, coordination, and self-confidence. We work with children, teenagers, and adults, helping everyone unlock their potential. SK Ratibor is not only about sports, but also a community where hard work, mutual support, and the pursuit of victory are valued.

Purple

initial mastery

Requirements

I. General physical preparedness.

  • Flexion and extension of arms (i.p. front support or on knees) – 10 times.
  • Trunk raising (i.p. lying on back, hands behind head or straight along the body) – 10 times.
  • Leg raising behind the head (i.p. lying on back, arms straight along the body) – 10 times.
  • Simultaneous trunk and leg lift (i.p. lying on stomach, arms straight along the body) – 10 times.
  • Pelvic lift (i.p. lying on back, legs bent, arms straight along the body) – 10 times.
  • Entering the plank in support on straight arms (i.p. standing) – 10 times.
  • Burpee complex, light version – 10 times.

II. Elements of gymnastics, acrobatics. Protection and self-protection during gymnastic and acrobatic exercises.

  • Side rolls (i.p. lying on back, arms up).
  • Backward rolls (i.p. sitting in grouping).
  • Fixation of the final position when falling to the side on the hip.
  • Roll from one side to the other with fixation of the final position when falling to the side.

III. Special technical preparedness.

1. Formation, commands.
  • Greeting, fall in, dress, attention, at ease.
  • Formation in a line, in a column, opening ranks.
2. Mastery of positions and stances.
  • Basic stance, fighting stance (left-sided, right-sided), frontal (educational) stance.
  • Kneeling stance, on the right (left) knee.
  • Parterre position.
  • Position lying on the stomach (arms forward, to the sides, along the body).
  • Position lying on the back (arms forward, to the sides, along the body, legs bent).
3. Mastery of hand and leg strike techniques in frontal stance in place and in motion.
  • Execution of straight hand strikes (left, right).
  • Execution of forward knee strikes (left, right).
  • Execution of 4 combinations consisting of two or more hand and leg strikes (based on studied material).
4. Mastery of defense techniques against hand strikes in frontal stance in place and in motion.
  • Execution of defensive parrying technique (block) top to bottom and bottom to top.
  • Execution of 2 combinations consisting of defense and strikes (based on studied material).
5. Mastery of movement techniques.
  • Movement in frontal stance with a closing step (forward-backward, right-left).
  • Movement on knees (i.p. stance on one knee).
  • Movement lying on the back, lying on the stomach.
  • Movement in a grip with a partner by step, closing step (forward-backward, right-left).
6. Mastery of wrestling techniques.
  • Grips by the wrists, by the arms under the elbows.
  • Grips by the kimono lapel and jacket sleeve (one-sided, two-sided).
  • Clothing grips on shoulders.
  • Clothing grips on the chest.
  • Crossed clothing grips on the chest.
7. Mastery of wrestling techniques in lying position, parterre.
  • Execution of side hold.
